Problem & Clinical Context

  • SCI rehab patients have long inpatient stays with daily OT/PT/RT/nursing documentation demands.
  • Clinicians often spend major time charting after sessions.
  • Documentation quality and continuity matter for safety, billing, and team communication.
Observed Need: 1 hour care session + significant post-session charting overhead.
Goal: assist documentation speed without sacrificing clinician control.

Background: Scribes & AI Scribes

  • Traditional scribes: improve throughput but costly and hard to scale.
  • Generic AI scribes: fast drafting, but often not rehab-specific and may hide uncertainty.
  • Rehab Scribe Pro difference: structured rehab template mapping + confidence alerts + mandatory human sign-off.
Core Safety Philosophy: AI drafts, clinicians decide.

Design Constraints & Compliance

  • HIPAA: protect PHI, limit access, secure transmission/storage.
  • Human oversight: no autonomous final documentation.
  • Auditability: preserve action history for accountability.
  • Role controls: nurse/physician/auditor permissions.
  • Risk transparency: low-confidence outputs must be visible and reviewable.

Discussion of Results

  • Prototype shows AI can reduce charting friction in rehab workflows.
  • Confidence-guided review keeps clinician attention on uncertain items.
  • Manual edits preserve clinical judgement and ownership.
  • Current version is MVP: still needs enterprise hosting, identity integration, and long-term validation.

Next Step 2: Integrate in a Real VA Rehab Clinic

  1. Stakeholder alignment: rehab chief, nursing leadership, OT/PT champions.
  2. Governance review: privacy, security, legal, AI governance.
